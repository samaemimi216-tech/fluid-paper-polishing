# Journal of Fluid Mechanics leaning

Center the manuscript on a fluid-mechanical question and the insight gained, even when computation or machine learning is essential.

- State the flow configuration, controlling parameters, and mechanism early.
- Use precise nondimensional ranges and diagnostics rather than broad application claims.
- Let the abstract move from question to observation and interpretation; include the method only to establish trust.
- Prefer compact section logic: formulation/setup, validation where needed, results organized by physical question, and conclusions.
- Treat generalization beyond the simulated regime conservatively.
