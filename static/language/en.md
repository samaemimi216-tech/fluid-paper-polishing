# English revision

- Prefer one main scientific action per sentence, but retain necessary equation-linked detail.
- Place conditions near the claim they limit.
- Use present tense for established knowledge, equations, figures, and method definitions; past tense for simulations, experiments, and observed results.
- Prefer concrete subjects: `the pressure gradient drives`, `the scheme recovers`, `the temperature profile approaches`.
- Avoid stacked nouns, dangling modifiers, repeated `it can be seen`, and vague referents such as `this phenomenon`.
- Use articles consistently for named equations, models, methods, domains, and quantities.
- Keep hyphenation consistent: finite-volume method, second-order accuracy, time-step constraint, grid-independent solution.
