# Claim-evidence calibration

| Wording | Use when |
|---|---|
| `recovers the ... limit` | analysis or convergence demonstrates the limiting equation/behavior |
| `preserves` | a discrete property is proved or quantified under stated conditions |
| `agrees with` | a named reference and error/uncertainty or defined visual comparison is available |
| `predicts` | output is compared with independent target data or a held-out reference |
| `reproduces` | a known structure, trend, or benchmark is recovered without implying independent prediction |
| `reveals` | the analysis exposes a previously unresolved pattern with direct evidence |
| `indicates` | evidence supports but does not uniquely establish the interpretation |
| `suggests` | evidence is indirect, limited, or exploratory |
| `may` / `could` | mechanism or extension remains plausible but unverified |

Red lines:

- Do not use `significant` for magnitude unless quantified or for statistics unless tested.
- Do not turn low error on one case into `robust`, `general`, or `multiscale`.
- Do not turn matched profiles into proof of mechanism.
- Do not equate strong scaling with weak scaling or throughput with algorithmic efficiency.
- Do not call learned correlations physical laws without independent tests.
