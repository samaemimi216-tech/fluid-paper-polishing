# Discussion

Structure the discussion as:

`main finding -> physical/numerical interpretation -> comparison with prior knowledge -> rival explanation -> limitation -> implication`

- Explain why the result occurs using balances, scales, structures, or controlled evidence.
- Distinguish numerical effects from physical effects and correlation from mechanism.
- State where the interpretation may fail: regime, geometry, closure, resolution, data support, or boundary condition.
- Do not replay every result or introduce unreported numerical evidence.
- Calibrate `demonstrates`, `indicates`, `suggests`, and `may` to evidence strength.
- Use `results from` only when the causal chain is established. Prefer
  `is consistent with`, `can be attributed to`, or `may arise from` when the
  evidence is diagnostic but not exclusive.
- Connect limitations to next-step work: additional regimes, grid/velocity-space
  resolution, experimental confirmation, uncertainty quantification, or
  out-of-distribution tests.
