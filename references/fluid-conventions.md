# General fluid conventions

- Define coordinates, velocity components, pressure, density, temperature, stress, heat flux, and reference scales consistently.
- Distinguish local, bulk, mean, instantaneous, phase-averaged, ensemble-averaged, and time-averaged quantities.
- State whether variables are dimensional or nondimensional.
- Name flow direction and surface orientation before using upstream/downstream, normal/tangential, inner/outer, or leading/trailing.
- Use `flow rate` for volumetric or mass throughput, `velocity` for a vector field, and `speed` for its magnitude.
- Use `pressure drop` across a defined interval and `pressure gradient` as a spatial derivative.
- Distinguish boundary-layer separation, recirculation, vortex formation, and generic flow reversal.
- Attach regime claims to a definition and parameter range; do not treat a single threshold as universal across geometries and models.
