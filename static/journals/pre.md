# Physical Review E leaning

Center the paper on a general physical or methodological question. Keep the introduction compact, make derivations and assumptions transparent, and connect numerical observations to physical interpretation or limiting behavior.

- For kinetic/LBM methods, state moment basis, forcing, equilibrium, and recovered equations precisely.
- Use focused canonical tests to isolate one property rather than presenting a long benchmark catalogue.
- Avoid application-heavy motivation unless it establishes the broader physics.
