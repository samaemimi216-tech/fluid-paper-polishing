# Heat-transfer and radiative-transport conventions

- Distinguish temperature, internal energy, heat flux, radiative intensity, incident radiation, and radiative source terms.
- Define optical thickness and the optically thin, intermediate, and thick regimes used in the study.
- Distinguish ballistic, diffusive, and transitional transport; reserve `asymptotic-preserving` for evidence that the discrete method approaches the limiting model without resolving the smallest scale.
- State gray/non-gray, absorbing/emitting/scattering, isotropic/anisotropic, and local-thermodynamic-equilibrium assumptions.
- For conjugate or combined transfer, state how radiation, conduction, convection, and material energy are coupled.
- Report normalized temperature and heat flux only after defining reference values.
