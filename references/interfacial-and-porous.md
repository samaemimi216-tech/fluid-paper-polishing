# Interfacial and porous-flow conventions

- Define phases, interface representation, wetting/contact-angle treatment, capillary number, density/viscosity ratio, and phase-change assumptions.
- Distinguish physical currents from spurious currents and equilibrium consistency from dynamic accuracy.
- For VOF, phase-field, SPH, or immersed-boundary methods, state how interface geometry, force balance, and conservation are evaluated.
- In porous media, distinguish pore-scale, Darcy/REV-scale, and upscaled quantities; define porosity, permeability, saturation, and averaging.
- Do not infer a pore-scale mechanism from a bulk trend without spatial or budget evidence.
