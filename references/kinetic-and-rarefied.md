# Kinetic and rarefied-flow conventions

- Define the Knudsen number, collision/relaxation model, distribution function, velocity-space quadrature, and macroscopic moments.
- Distinguish continuum, slip, transition, and free-molecular regimes only under the adopted convention.
- Use `recovers the Navier-Stokes limit` for a demonstrated asymptotic limit, not merely a matching profile.
- Explain whether transport and collision are split or coupled and how this affects the time-step and mesh constraints.
- For UGKS/DUGKS/LBM-type methods, separate evolution, reconstruction, collision, moment update, and boundary treatment.
- State whether conservation is exact at the discrete level or satisfied within numerical tolerance.
- Compare with DSMC using compatible molecular models, boundary accommodation, sampling, and uncertainty.
