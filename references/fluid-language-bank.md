# Fluid-mechanics language bank

Use these as semantic patterns, not canned phrases.

## Method actions

- formulate, derive, discretize, reconstruct, integrate, couple, recover, preserve, resolve, impose, update, evaluate, constrain, decompose, upscale

## Evidence actions

- verify against an analytical solution
- reproduce the reference profile
- converge at the expected rate
- remain bounded over the tested range
- conserve mass to within the reported tolerance
- agree with measurements within uncertainty
- reduce the error or cost under matched conditions
- preserve the equilibrium state to within the reported residual
- retain parallel efficiency under weak scaling
- generalize to the held-out Reynolds-number or geometry range

## Flow and transport actions

- accelerates, decelerates, impinges, separates, reattaches, recirculates, diffuses, convects, relaxes, scatters, attenuates
- the peak shifts toward, the layer thickens, the gradient steepens, the wake broadens, the flux approaches the diffusive limit
- particles preferentially concentrate, the interface relaxes, the recirculation region extends, the spectrum collapses, the closure remains realizable

## Calibrated interpretation

- `results from` for established causal evidence
- `is attributed to` for a supported interpretation
- `is consistent with` for compatible but non-exclusive evidence
- `may arise from` for a tentative mechanism

## Comparison syntax

- `under the same mesh, time step, and stopping criterion`
- `the methods differ in the treatment of ...`
- `the gain in accuracy is accompanied by ...`
- `method A is preferable in ..., whereas method B is advantageous when ...`

Avoid `superior`, `excellent`, `remarkable`, and `promising` without a named metric and comparison boundary.
